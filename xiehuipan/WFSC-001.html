The Wilhelm Furtwängler Society of China expects to release its first Society disc after its establishment

WFSC-001 Beethoven: Symphony No. 5

          The Wilhelm Furtwängler Society of China expects to release its first Society disc after its establishment - WFSC-001 - on 1 September 2021, featuring Beethoven's Symphony No. 5 performed by Wilhelm Furtwängler conducting the Berlin Philharmonic in 1943. We have chosen to transfer two LP sets published in the early Soviet Union (1959), issued by two publishers, Lighthouse and Riga. Of these, we chose to restore the Riga transfer to make the sound more natural and listenable, and to largely remove the noise floor. For the Lighthouse transfer, we chose to retain its original characteristics, so that music lovers can hear a sound that is closer to the master tapes.

          The booklet for this CD release is designed to be in both Chinese and English and is over ten pages thick. It is quite informative. It includes, but is not limited to, the historical context of the recording, the recording equipment and related information, as well as Furtwängler's analysis and comments on his interpretation of Beethoven's Fifth Symphony. Stay tuned!

          As for the price of the CD, WFSC will always offer it at an affordable price to Furtwänglerians. The CD is priced at ¥60 and shipping fee is free with the purchase of three or more copies, with individual purchases of no more than five copies.

Note: This CD is an internal production of the Society. The Society is non-profit making and all profits from the CD will go into the ongoing operation of the Society.
